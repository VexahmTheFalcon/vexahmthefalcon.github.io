GDPR Requirements for Bloggers in 2026

Last updated: February 2026

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies to websites that process personal data of individuals located in the European Union. Bloggers serving EU visitors may fall within its scope depending on how personal data and tracking technologies are used.

Do Bloggers Need to Comply with GDPR?

If a blog collects personal data — including through contact forms, analytics tools, advertising cookies, or newsletter signups — GDPR obligations may apply when EU residents are involved.

Even small blogs using analytics or advertising platforms often process personal data indirectly.

Cookie Consent Requirements

Under GDPR and the ePrivacy Directive, non-essential cookies generally require prior user consent before being set. This includes advertising and analytics cookies in many configurations.

A compliant implementation typically involves:

  • A visible consent banner
  • Clear explanation of cookie categories
  • Ability to accept or reject non-essential cookies
  • Documentation of user consent where required

Privacy Policy Transparency

Websites must provide clear information about what data is collected, how it is used, and which third parties process it.

Are Cookie Consent Tools Mandatory?

While no specific software is mandated by law, many website owners use consent management platforms (CMPs) to implement structured consent collection.

If using Google Analytics or Ads, understanding how consent signals affect data collection is important. See: Google Consent Mode v2 Explained.
